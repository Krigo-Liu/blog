Example:

If f:[a,b]→R is continuous, then its graph {(x,f(x))} has measure zero.

Proof:

Continuous functions are Riemann integrable: by Riemann's criterion for any ϵ>0, we choose a partition P={a=x0,x1,...,xn=b} such that, $$U(f,P)-L(f,P)=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} (M_{i}-m_{i})(x_{i}-x_{i-1})<\epsilon/2$$ (If we take the ith rectangle to be [xi−1,xi]×[mi,Mi], then these closed recatanges covers the graph, with total area less than ϵ/2. By slightly enlarging () them, we can get a cover by open rectangles with total area at most ϵ. Therefore the graph has measure zero.)
