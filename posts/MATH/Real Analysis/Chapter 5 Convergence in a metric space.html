Definition - 1:

Let (M,d) be a metric space, and consider a sequence in M,{x(k)}k=1∞. Then, the {x(k)} converges to x∈M, which denoted by limk→∞x(k)=x or x(k)→x. If for all ϵ>0, there exists natural number N(ϵ) such that , for all n≥N , $$d(x^{(k)},x)<\epsilon$$

  • 这里证明收敛的核心逻辑： ^adaf3a
    • 目前有一个metric space - (M,d).
    • 我们构造一个 sequence - {x(k)} in M, 并且说明此数列收敛到 x, x∈M.
      • 这个收敛我们用 limk→∞=x or x(k)→x 表示
    • 然后表示 for all ϵ>0, there exists N(ϵ), (注意这里的 N 是用 ϵ 表达的) then for all n≥N , $$d(x^{(k)},x)<\epsilon$$

Examples:

  • In R, $$(-\frac{1}{k})^{\infty}_{k=1} \rightarrow0$$
  • In R2 with l2 metric, the sequence {(1k,1k)} converges to (0,0) as follows: Let ϵ>0 be arbitrary. Ley N>2ϵ . Then, whenever k≥N, $$d\left(\left(\frac{1}{k},\frac{1}{k}\right),(0,0)\right)= \sqrt{\frac{1}{k^{2}}+ \frac{1}{k^{2}}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{k}<\epsilon$$

Some facts

Limits are unique, if there exist limit:

In (M,d), if (xk)→y and (xk)→z, then y=z .

  • Proof: Let ϵ>0 be arbitrary. Since (xk)→y and (xk)→z, we can choose N1 and N2 such that whenever n≥N1,d(xk,y)=d(y,xk)<ϵ ; whenever n≥N2,d(xk,z)<ϵ . Then, let N=max(N1,N2). $$d(y,x)\leq d(y,x_{k})+d(x_{k},z)<\epsilon+\epsilon = 2\epsilon$$ Since ϵ>0 is arbitrary, and by the metric space definition: d(y,z)≥0 , then $$d(y,z)=0$$

Definition of bounded sequence:

Definition - 2:

In (M,d): there exists a positive K>0 and a point p=0→ such that for all terms of the sequence, $$d(x_{k},p)\leq K$$ 理解：如果一个空间里面的所有序列的点（xk）距离零向量 0→ 的距离小于某一个大于零数。感觉可以用 “存在上界” 概括。

Statement:

  • If {x(k)} is a sequence of points in M with respect to the metric d, then {x(k)} is bounded in (M,d).
    • Proof: Let p∈M be a point. Let x(k)→x, and call L=d(x,p). Choose ϵ0=1, then for some natural number N and all k≤N,d(x(k),x)<1=ϵ. Then, $$d(x^{(k)},x)\leq d(x^{(k)},x)+d(x,p)<1+L$$ Therefore if we choose $$K=\max(d(x{(1)},p),...,d(x,p),1+L)$$then the distance from any x(k) to the point p will be bounded above by K as required.

Rn together with l1,l2,l∞ metric

Theorem:

a sequence of points x(k) in Rn converges to x in l2 distance if and only if $$\lim_{k\rightarrow \infty}x^{(k)}{i} = x(\forall i=1,2,...,n).$$

  • Lemma: In Rn, for all i, $$\sum_{i=1}^{n}|v_{i}| = ||v||{1}\geq ||v||\geq||v||{\infty} = \max|v_{i}|\geq|v_{i}|.$$
  • Proof of theorem: First, suppose x(k)→x in 2-norm. Then ∀ϵ>0,∃N,∀k≥N, $$|x_{i}^{(k)}-x_{i}|\leq {lemma}||x-x||{2}<^{conv}\epsilon \Rightarrow \lim{k\rightarrow \infty}x^{(k)}{i} = x$$ Next , suppose for all i and for all ϵ>0,limk→∞xi(k)=xi. Then for each i, there exists Ni such that whenever k≥Ni,|xi(k)−xi<ϵn|. Then, taking N=max1≤i≤n(Ni), whenever k≥N, $$||x^{(k)}-x ||{2}\leq ||x^{(k)}-x ||=\sum\limits_{i=1}{n}|x_{i}-x_{i}|<\frac{n\epsilon}{n} = \epsilon$$Therefore we conclude that limk→∞x(k)=x in l2-norm.
