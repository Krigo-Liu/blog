Chapter 6 Uniformly continuity

Definition:

  • Key property of continuous functions on compact space. f:X→Y is uniformly continuous if: $$\forall\epsilon>0,\exists\delta_{\epsilon}>0,d_{X}(x_{1},x_{2})<\delta \Rightarrow d_{Y}(f(x_{1}),f(x_{2}))<\epsilon$$

Theorem:

Let f:K→Y be a continuous function, where K is compact. Then f is uniformly continuous.

  • Proof 1: by using open-cover compactness

  • Proof 2: by using sequential compactness and contradiction.

前情提要：

Uniform contiinuous functions and related concepts:

Continuous ⊃ uniformly continuous ⊃ α-Holder continuous ⊃ Lipschitz continuous ⊃ contraction mapping

α-Holder continuous:

For some α<(0,1] and L>0, for all x,y∈X, $$d(f(x),f(y))\leq L\cdot [d(x,y)]^{\alpha}$$where f:X→Y is a continuous map between metric spaces.

Lipschitz continuous:

For some Lipschitz constant L>0 and all x,y∈X, $$d(f(x),f(y))\leq L\cdot d(x,y)$$where f:X→Y is a continuous map between metric spaces.

Constraction mapping:

d(f(x),f(y))≤rd(x,y)$$forsome$0<r<1$.
