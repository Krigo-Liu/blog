Definition - 1

Let M be a set. A metric on M is a function d:M×M→R which statisfies: for all x,y,z∈M,

  1. d(x,y)≥0, if and only if x=y→d(x,y)=0
  2. d(x,y)=d(y,x)
  3. d(x,z)≤d(x,y)+d(y,z)

Definition - 2

A metric space is a set M together with a metric d− metric space, where d we often think as a distance function.

Examples:

In the (R,d)

where d(x,y)=|x−y|

In the R2

For the l2 (usual / Euclidean) metric: $$d(x,y)=\sqrt{(x_{1}-y_{1}){2}+(x_{2}+y_{2}){2}}$$

In the R3

  • For the l1 metric: $$d(x,y)=|x_{1}-y_{1}|+|x_{2} - y_{2} |$$
  • For the l∞ metric: $$d(x,y)=\sup(|x_{1}-y_{1}|,|x_{2} - y_{2} |,|x_{3}-y_{3}|)$$
